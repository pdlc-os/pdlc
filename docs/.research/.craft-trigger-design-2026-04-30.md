**Date:** 2026-04-30
**Status:** Ideation only — alignment on direction (Option C) but no implementation. Captured here so the context survives `/compact`.

This doc captures everything decided + everything still open about the future "craft" trigger — the automatic sub-step(s) that fire when PDLC detects design ambiguity (Inception-side) or as-built drift (Construction-side) and wants to generate visual variants for the team to converge on.

## What's decided

### 1. Naming and framing — automatic step, NOT user-initiated command

**Original integration plan §10.3** sketched a `/pdlc craft` slash command. **The user explicitly rejected user-initiated framing** during the Wave 7 conversation (2026-04-30): *"In the PDLC flow, we have steps that lead to other steps and handoffs are automatic."* So craft becomes an **automatic sub-step within an existing phase**, triggered by detected conditions, not a slash command the user runs.

The user-facing surface remains `pdlc livemode` (the visual portal) — when craft is running, the portal multiplexes its content automatically (Wave 0 already laid the multiplexing groundwork; the manifest schema supports `kind: "craft"`).

**Internal name candidates** (to be picked when implementation starts):
- "Variant Convergence" — descriptive
- "Variant Roundtable" — fits the Roundtable pattern (Design-Laws Roundtable from Wave 2)
- "Visual Convergence Pass" — clearest about what it does

Lean: **Variant Convergence** for the step name; the helper file might keep "craft" in its slug for continuity with the integration-plan vocabulary.

### 2. Option C — both Inception-side and Construction-side triggers

The user picked **Option C** from the trigger ideation menu I presented in the Wave 7 messages. Three options were on the table:

| Option | Trigger location | Lean reason |
|---|---|---|
| A | Construction-side only — after Party Review when Muse's as-built scorecard shows a significant negative delta | Sharpest trigger signal (real impl vs design) |
| B | Inception-side only — after Step 10.6 when the Design-Laws Audit can't converge | Cheapest fix — design revisions before code |
| **C** | **Both — Inception-side for design-time ambiguity, Construction-side for as-built drift** | **Most powerful, matches PDLC's existing "catch issues at every phase" posture; user picked this** |

The user's framing: **same problem can surface at two phases, fire the right intervention at each.**

### 3. Step numbering placement

| Phase | New step | Position | Lead |
|---|---|---|---|
| Inception (Brainstorm) | **Step 10.7 — Variant Convergence** | After 10.6 (Design-Laws Audit), before 11 (PRD link updates) | Muse-led |
| Construction (Build) | **Step 12.5 — Variant Convergence** | After 12 (Party Review), before 13 (Review approval gate) | Muse + Friday joint |

This mirrors the established pattern (10.5 Threat Modeling, 10.6 Design-Laws Audit, 11.5 UX Verify) — sub-numbered steps for conditional gates that fire mid-phase.

### 4. Trigger conditions (first-draft, calibration needed)

**Inception-side (Step 10.7) — fires when ALL of:**
- Step 10.6 ran with **Full** triage (audit was substantive, not just lite or skipped), AND
- One or more of these signals:
  - Heuristic 8 (Aesthetic & Minimalism) score ≤ 2 AND the affected component is a major user-facing surface
  - Heuristic 4 (Consistency & Standards) score ≤ 2 AND affects a primary flow
  - The Design-Laws Roundtable cross-talk failed to converge in 3 rounds (locked positions on a P1+ finding)
  - Two or more P1 findings tagged "needs visual exploration to resolve"

**Construction-side (Step 12.5) — fires when ALL of:**
- ux-review.md exists with Lite/Full triage (Step 10.6 ran), AND
- Muse's as-built scorecard at Party Review shows:
  - Nielsen 8 (Aesthetic & Minimalism) drops ≥3 points vs design-time, OR
  - Nielsen 4 (Consistency & Standards) drops ≥3 points vs design-time, OR
  - Total Nielsen drops ≥5 points vs design-time
- AND the affected component is a major user-facing surface (not an admin tool tweak)

**Calibration plan** (per integration plan §8 Q1 pattern): ship conservative thresholds; tighten after first 3 features run through them. The thresholds are guesses that need ground-truth data.

### 5. Lead and participants

| Step | Lead | Participants |
|---|---|---|
| 10.7 (Inception-side) | Muse solo | Friday consults on feasibility if a variant requires source rewriting; otherwise Muse alone |
| 12.5 (Construction-side) | Muse + Friday joint | Muse drives variant criteria, Friday writes the variants in branches; full Party Review team available for cross-talk on the chosen variant |

The Muse + Friday joint lead at 12.5 is unusual for PDLC (most steps have a single lead). Precedent: the existing pattern has clear sole leads (Phantom for 10.5, Muse for 10.6, Pulse for Verify). Joint lead may need a structural decision — most likely Muse drives convention/heuristic criteria and Friday drives source generation; explicit roles avoid lead ambiguity.

### 6. Output artifact

Findings + variant choice + final convergence appended to **`docs/pdlc/design/[feature-name]/ux-review.md`** (the same artifact used by Steps 10.6, Construction Review, Ship Verify). One file per feature, four lifecycle moments captured (10.6 design-time → 10.7 design-variant convergence → as-built at Construction Review → ship-verify). Template version bumps to **1.4.0** when this section ships.

Section name: **"Variant Convergence"** (or two sub-sections if Inception and Construction outputs need to be distinguished — TBD).

### 7. What's already in place that supports craft

- **Wave 0 visual portal** — proxies to whichever backend is active. Already supports `kind: "craft"` in the manifest schema (just needs a craft backend to register it).
- **`pdlc livemode` CLI** — backend-agnostic; will surface craft variants without rename.
- **`ux-review.md` template** — already structured to accept new sub-sections; adding Variant Convergence is additive.
- **The integration plan §10.3 architectural decision** — separate backends (visual companion ≠ live-class server) means craft can stand up its own backend without entangling the visual companion's plumbing.
- **The Wave-3 as-built scorecard** — provides the trigger signal for Step 12.5. Without Wave 3, Step 12.5's automatic-detection wouldn't have data to fire on.

So **a substantial fraction of the craft infrastructure is already shipped** — the missing piece is the trigger logic, the variant-generation mechanism, and the skill files.

## What's NOT decided — open design choices to ideate on

### Q1. One skill file or two?

**Option (a):** Two skill files — `skills/brainstorm/steps/variant-convergence.md` (Step 10.7) and `skills/build/steps/variant-convergence.md` (Step 12.5), with shared helper logic in a third file.

**Option (b):** One skill file with phase-aware behavior (e.g., `skills/shared/variant-convergence.md` that's loaded by both 03-design.md and 03-review.md).

**Tradeoff:** (a) is clearer (the file path tells you which phase it fires in); (b) avoids drift between the two implementations. PDLC's existing pattern leans (a) — `threat-model.md` lives in brainstorm/steps/ and is unambiguously a brainstorm-side skill.

**Lean:** (a) — two files, accept some duplication, keep the path → phase mapping clear.

### Q2. Variant generation — branch-based MVP or live-class source-rewriting

**Option (a) — Branch-based MVP:** Friday writes 2-3 alternative branches (e.g., `feature/X-variant-a`, `feature/X-variant-b`, `feature/X-variant-c`) in the same repo. Muse renders each in the existing visual companion (different screen files per variant). Team picks one in the browser. Friday merges the chosen variant back to the feature branch; the rest get deleted.

- Pros: ships without a new backend; uses existing visual companion machinery; minimal new infrastructure.
- Cons: branches are heavyweight; Friday writes 3× the code; merge cost on the rejected branches.

**Option (b) — Live-class source-rewriting:** Build the `live`-class server (per integration plan §10.3) that runs against the user's actual running app, rewrites source in-place, and shows variants live. impeccable already has this; we'd port the architectural pattern but write our own bundle (don't reuse impeccable's 194KB live-browser.js).

- Pros: variants update in real time; matches the "craft" branding from the integration plan.
- Cons: substantial new infrastructure (live-server.cjs + browser-side variant picker + source-rewrite plumbing); license-compliance work; weeks of effort.

**Lean:** **(a) for v1**, (b) when (a) hits a real ceiling. v1 ships value cheaply; v2 is a real-source-rewriting upgrade only if the team actually hits the ceiling of what branch-based exploration can cover.

### Q3. Calibration thresholds

The trigger thresholds in §4 above are guesses. The plan §8 Q1 already has precedent for this: *"implement Wave 2 with the questions as specified, refine after the first 3 features run through it."* Same approach for Step 10.7 / 12.5 — ship conservative, tighten via real-feature feedback.

Specifically untested:
- "Nielsen 8 drops ≥3" — is 3 too low (fires too often)? too high (misses real cases)?
- "Major user-facing surface" — what counts? A primary CTA? A first-experience screen? Need a working definition.
- "Cross-talk failed to converge in 3 rounds" — is this the right escalation signal vs the existing pitch+vote?

**Plan:** ship Wave 8 with these as documented defaults; add a calibration log to `docs/pdlc/memory/METRICS.md` that records when craft fired and whether the variant exploration was useful (Y/N + 1-line note). After 3 features, re-tune.

### Q4. Lead-agent assignment for joint Muse + Friday at Step 12.5

PDLC's existing pattern has unambiguous sole leads. Joint lead is novel. Three structural options:

**(i) Co-lead with explicit role split:** Muse owns variant criteria + convention/heuristic decisions; Friday owns source generation + technical feasibility. Two handoff banners (Neo→Muse for criteria, Muse→Friday for generation, Friday→Muse for review, Muse→Neo at close).

**(ii) Sequential single-lead:** Step 12.5 is split into 12.5a (Muse-led — define variant criteria) and 12.5b (Friday-led — generate variants per criteria) and 12.5c (joint review). Each sub-step has a single lead.

**(iii) Muse-lead with Friday as participant:** Muse leads end-to-end; Friday participates as a contributor (like Echo/Phantom in Party Review). Cleanest single-lead pattern.

**Lean:** (iii) — Muse leads; Friday contributes. Matches PDLC's existing "lead + participants" shape. The handoffs stay clean (Neo→Muse→Neo).

### Q5. Output artifact — same ux-review.md or separate variant-record.md?

**Option (a):** All four lifecycle moments (10.6 audit, 10.7 variant convergence, Construction as-built, Ship Verify) live in the same `ux-review.md`. Already-precedent-set in Waves 2-4.

**Option (b):** Variants are a separate concern; create `variants.md` per feature for the variant-generation history.

**Lean:** **(a) — same file, new section.** Continuity wins; fewer files; Reflect-phase aggregation is simpler. Adds a *Variant Convergence* section per phase (10.7 and 12.5 each get a sub-section).

### Q6. UX for the variant chooser in the browser

When variants are rendered in the visual companion, how does the team pick one?

**Option (a):** Side-by-side rendering, one chooser button per variant. Pure visual choice.

**Option (b):** Sequential rendering (one variant at a time, click "next variant"), final pick on a summary page.

**Option (c):** Side-by-side with annotation enabled per variant — team can mark up each one, then pick.

**Lean:** **(a) for v1** with **(c) as a follow-on** when annotation per variant becomes useful (the annotation overlay is already in place from Wave 7a; extending to multi-variant is incremental).

### Q7. What happens when a variant gets accepted

After the team picks a variant:

- **Inception-side (10.7):** the chosen variant updates the design docs (`ARCHITECTURE.md`, `data-model.md`, `api-contracts.md`, the user-flow descriptions in PRD). The other variants are noted in `ux-review.md` as "considered alternatives." Continue to Step 11.
- **Construction-side (12.5):** Friday merges the chosen variant's branch back to the feature branch; the others get deleted. The as-built scorecard re-runs; Party Review re-evaluates. Continue to Step 13 (Review approval).

The 12.5 case has a re-loop: variant accepted → Party Review re-runs → if scorecard now passes, continue; if still drifted, escalate to human review.
